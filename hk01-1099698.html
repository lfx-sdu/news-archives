Standing in 2025 and looking at the Made in China 2025 high-score answer sheet, it makes the West even more blushing

In 2015, the "Government Work Report" first proposed the "Made in China 2025" Hongda plan, and then the executive meeting of the State Council deliberated and approved the "Made in China 2025". When the "2025 Made in China" was proposed, it seemed that this ideal was still some time away from us. In the blink of an eye, 10 years have passed. In the past 10 years of silence on "Made in China 2025", what changes have taken place?

The goal of "Made in China 2025" is simply to transform China from a manufacturing power into a manufacturing powerhouse, achieving technological self-sufficiency and reducing dependence on foreign technology and supply chains. At the time, China was at the lower end of the global industrial value chain, producing mostly cheap and technologically backward products, and Made in China 2025 aims to change that.

The State Council has listed the key areas of "Made in China 2025" to vigorously promote breakthrough development: a new generation of information technology industry, high-end CNC machines and robots, aerospace equipment, marine engineering equipment and high-tech ships, advanced rail transit equipment, energy-saving and new energy vehicles, power equipment, agricultural machinery equipment, new materials, biomedicine and high-performance medical equipment.

U.S. Secretary of State Marco Rubio visits Panama to meet with President Ma Jose Raul Mulino on February 2, 2025. （Reuters）

When Secretary of State Marco Rubio was a senator from Florida in 2019, he published a special report on "Made in China 2025 and the Future of American Industry", and in September last year, he published a sequel to the report, "A World Built by China: Nine Years After Made in China 2025".(The World China Made:"Made in China 2025" Nine Years Later) pointed out that nine of the 10 key industry targets set by China have been basically achieved. These achievements cover a wide range of core fields such as new energy vehicles, information technology, aerospace, and power equipment, injecting strong impetus into the overall improvement of China's manufacturing industry. There is also another media summary, Made in China 2025, a total of 36 major projects are planned, and 35 have been completed or basically completed on schedule, basically leaving chips.

According to the progress chart of "Made in China 2025" released by Bloomberg, the industries that are currently in the leading position (Global Leader) in China are: drones, solar energy, graphene, high-speed rail, and electric vehicles. In the past, two areas became global leaders. Industries with global competitiveness include: semiconductors, artificial intelligence, robotics, machines, pharmaceuticals, and large LNG carriers.

In the past 10 years, six areas have gone from being globally backward to becoming globally competitive, with impressive progress. Semiconductors, machines and pharmaceuticals, which are often criticized, are considered by Bloomberg to be globally competitive. The only industry behind is the commercial aircraft, but it will also be globally competitive by 2030.

In other words, no matter how it is divided, Made in China 2025 has a very high degree of completion.

Broadly speaking, the goal is to increase China's share of global manufacturing value added. As of 2023, China's share of global manufacturing value added has risen from 26% in 2015 to 29%. The plan aims to increase R&D spending to 2.5% of GDP by 2025. This goal has already been achieved ahead of schedule in 2023.

In terms of fields, the most outstanding are energy-saving and new energy vehicles, and power equipment. China's goal is to become a leader in clean energy technology. Chinese companies now produce about 90 percent of the world's solar panels and 70 percent of the world's batteries, up from 65 percent and 47 percent respectively in 2015. This field can be said to be a great success.

In terms of automobiles, China's new energy vehicles account for 70% of the world's production capacity. The original plan was to sell 3 million electric vehicles by 2025. But in fact, sales in 2024 will exceed 10 million units, accounting for nearly two-thirds of the global market. China's auto exports have become the world's largest and are growing rapidly, and it is likely to become China's largest export. Lithium-ion batteries also account for more than 70% of the world's total.

China's new energy vehicles account for 70% of the world's production capacity, and sales will exceed 10 million units in 2024, accounting for nearly two-thirds of the global market. （Getty Images）

In terms of power equipment, China ranks first in the world in photovoltaic panels, wind turbines, hydropower, thermal power, third-generation nuclear power, and fourth-generation nuclear power, and China is the only one in terms of fourth-generation high-temperature gas-cooled nuclear power technology. The world has a monopoly on ultra-high voltage transmission, and China has completely independent intellectual property rights, and even international standards are China's business. For example, the Sichuan-Chongqing 1000 kV UHV AC project is the first high-altitude UHV AC project, which has overcome the key technologies of high-altitude UHV such as air gap, external insulation, and electromagnetic environment. According to the data released by the World Energy Statistical Yearbook 2024, in 2023, the total global power generation will be 299248 billion kWh, and China's total power generation will reach 9,456.4 billion kWh, accounting for 31.60% of the world's total power generation. China's power generation will exceed 10 trillion kWh in 2024.

In terms of high-end CNC machines and robots, China's robotics industry will grow in 2023, accounting for 50% of the world's total, and Yushu Technology's robot dogs will have a global market share of more than 70%. China is undoubtedly the world's first-echelon country in the field of robotics. CNC machine bed has always been known as the mother of industry, and it is also a key project for the country to constantly pursue breakthroughs. China is already the world's largest producer and consumer of CNC machines, with a total output value of 32% of the world's.

From a technical point of view, in the five-axis linkage CNC machine bed, CNC heavy machine bed, vertical horizontal machining center, CNC lathe, CNC gear processing machine bed and other parts, the technology has reached the world's advanced level, breaking the foreign monopoly. However, there is still a gap between the overall technological development and the top level, such as in terms of accuracy retention and stability of the numerical control system.

CCTV economic information broadcast reported that the application of the Beidou system will help accelerate the new quality of productivity. (Xinhuanet)

In terms of aerospace equipment, the Beidou navigation system has been fully completed. China's space station has been built and operated independently, and personnel turnover is normalized. Network satellites have already begun to be networked. China's rocket capabilities are world-class.

J-20, Y-20, Z-20, J-35 are in full service, the first flight of the double sixth-generation aircraft, and the C919 is delivered on a large scale. China's patent applications in the field of UAV aerospace account for 70% of the world's total, and it is a proper first-echelon leader, and the UAV production represented by DJI has reached 70% of the global UAV market. In 2024, China will launch a total of 68 launch vehicles, with a success rate of 97.06%.

In addition to the previously exposed J-36 (see photo) and J-50 6th generation stealth fighters, US satellite photos show that there is also a new 6th generation fighter. (Weibo)

In terms of marine engineering equipment and high-tech ships, there have been significant breakthroughs in technology in the progress of marine engineering equipment, such as the 400,000-ton FPSO project using a number of advanced technologies, and the quality and accuracy control has reached the world's advanced level. In terms of shipbuilding, China already has the three most difficult shipbuilding technologies to produce LNG, luxury cruise ships, and large ocean-going cargo ships. China has topped the global offshore order list for five consecutive years, and a total of 73 offshore orders will be received in 2023. In 2023, China's shipbuilding completions, new orders, and hand-held orders will account for 49.7%, 67%, and 54.4% of the world's market share, respectively, and the market share of large-scale offshore equipment will continue to maintain a leading position in the world. Chinese shipbuilding 2024 already accounts for 70% of global orders.

In terms of high-speed rail technology, China has built a 45,000-kilometer high-speed railway network, accounting for more than 50% of the world's total mileage, making it the country with the longest operating mileage of high-speed railways in the world, and high-speed rail technology and services are also exported to Indonesia and other countries. Recently, China's high-speed rail has further completed the speed test, with a maximum speed of 450 kilometers per hour, and a new generation of maglev high-speed railway with a speed of 1,000 kilometers per hour has also been tested in Shanxi.

At present, China's high-speed rail mileage ranks first in the world, and it is far behind other countries, accounting for more than 50% of the world's total mileage. (Image source: Visual China)

In addition, in 2023, the scale of China's agricultural machinery and equipment industry will reach 293.71 billion yuan, with an export value of 6.36 billion US dollars. It has also achieved results in the research and development of a number of emerging materials, maintaining a catch-up or leading position in the forefront of 128 fields around the world, and new materials are widely used in various fields, and the output value is gradually emerging. In terms of biomedicine and high-performance medical devices, Chinese companies have been able to independently design and manufacture the world's most advanced superconducting magnetic resonance systems, some anti-cancer drugs have begun to enter the U.S. market, China's self-sufficiency in the field of biomedicine and high-performance medical devices has been enhanced, and the value of industrial output has been continuously improved. High-end medical devices and some key new materials and other fields are still dependent on imports, and the self-sufficiency rate is low, which is the direction that needs to be focused on breakthroughs in the future.

In terms of information technology, China has built the world's most advanced 5G equipment manufacturing industry chain and 5G network, with more than 4 million 5G base stations, nearly 20 times that of the United States. The semiconductor equipment industry chain has basically begun to be completed. The independent system has been successful, and the server system has accounted for more than half of the Chinese market last year. For low-end chips above 28nm, China has the world's largest production capacity. Of course, domestic semiconductor production capacity still needs to be improved, especially for high-end chips. At present, the main breakthrough in this field should be that key equipment and materials, including lithography machines, are still affected by Western export restrictions.

Looking back 10 years, "Made in China 2025" is no longer a grand plan on paper, and it has to be said that the sudden national stop mentioning "Made in China 2025" reflects a broader political strategy. First, controversy was avoided, and further international concerns and protectionist reactions were avoided. At the same time, this silence underscores China's strategic pragmatism, adapting its approach while achieving long-term economic goals. It has to be said that the style of doing things at the top of the central government fully demonstrates the subtlety of oriental culture. This implication shows two characteristics: insurance, conservatism.

In 2015, "Made in China 2025" was unveiled with a high profile, and then it was immediately silent. In the next 10 years, the term almost disappeared. For a long time, it made the Chinese people feel aggrieved and even guilty. Because you have to endure and let go. But now, although the international situation has changed abruptly in recent years and suffered a century-long pattern fission, China's international status and comprehensive strength have been greatly improved. But standing at the height of the country, in the contest of top high-tech, it is completely correct to be conservative and low-key.

Unitree Technology's humanoid robot appeared in the 2025 Spring Festival Gala performance program "Yang Bot".

It has to be said that now the "Made in China 2025" plan has achieved remarkable phased results, and the rise of China's science and technology has formed a "triple helix" response: the combination of government guidance + market drive + engineer dividends, which is rewriting the traditional division of labor of "Western R&D - Made in China". As the German magazine Der Spiegel put it: "This is not a simple industrial competition, but a technological paradigm shift at the civilizational level".

Indeed, as a direct result of the persistence and promotion of the "Made in China 2025" plan, Chinese society has undergone profound changes: Chinese consumers have now purchased more electric vehicles than fossil fuel vehicles, and the most popular ones are local brands such as BYD and Xiaomi; Chinese-made C919 airliners have begun to serve some of the busiest routes; The widespread adoption of 5G technology allows rail passengers to enjoy high-speed networks even when passing through tunnels; has more smart factories and automated terminals than any other country; The market size of mobile phones and computers using domestic chips and systems is expanding; China's shipbuilding capacity has exceeded that of the United States by more than 200 times......

And that's just the beginning. In fact, China not only has Made in China 2025, but also Chinese Standard 2035. If Made in China 2025 is to improve China's manufacturing capacity, then China Standard 2035 is to give China a say in the formulation of international technical standards. "Made in China 2025" focuses on improving China's manufacturing capacity and technological self-sufficiency, while "China Standard 2035" seeks to capitalize on these achievements by setting global technical standards. Every step of the way will have far-reaching implications for trade, technology and international relations. In 2023, Xi Jinping proposed "new quality productivity" on this basis.

A Huawei store in Beijing on August 30, 2023. （Reuters）

"Made in China 2025" is the catalyst for the rise of China's industry, and "China Standard 2035" indicates a more complex and ambitious strategy, with a new focus on establishing global technical standards, which marks a shift in strategic priorities, not just to make products, but also to define how products are manufactured and used on a global scale. As for the proposal of "new quality productivity", it is the quality of advanced productivity born from further technological revolutionary breakthroughs, innovative allocation of production factors, and in-depth industrial transformation and upgrading. The so-called high-quality development no longer stays at the level of utensils, but also leverages the optimization of laborers, labor materials, and labor objects.

Of course, it will be more difficult for China's manufacturing industry to continue to break through in the future. It must be noted that the West has put more pressure on Chinese manufacturing in technology and market. The West could launch a new round of trade wars and sanctions at any time in the face of high-quality, low-cost high-tech products from China, especially in photovoltaics, large wind turbines and electric vehicles. Although looking at the restrictive measures taken by the West so far, the effectiveness is limited and sometimes even counterproductive. However, in the face of the escalation of its measures, it should be treated differently and should not be taken lightly.

At the same time, various countries have also introduced various industrial plans, such as the Japanese Ministry of Economy, Trade and Industry launched the manufacturing digital upgrade plan, France also launched the "New Industrial France" plan, the United States' "Chips and Science Act" and "Inflation Reduction Act", the European Union also passed the "National Aid Plan" and the "Chips Act", etc., which means that the future manufacturing international competition is much more fierce than in the previous 10 years.
